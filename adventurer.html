ADVENTURER

I explore the edges of the world: off-road, under the sea, and through the sky.

• • •

I fly planes, ride motorcycles, and dive into wild places. I move through the world to better understand my place in it. Adventure isn't an escape — it's how I learn, integrate, and stay awake to life.

Flying

127 flight hours logged
Started with a discovery flight that changed everything. The moment the plane lifted off, I knew I had to learn to control this machine.

Earned my private pilot license flying Cessnas out of small airports. There's something pure about reading the wind, watching the instruments, and navigating by sight.

Now I fly whenever I can — for the perspective it gives, the focus it demands, and the reminder that humans really can soar.

Skydiving

6 jumps completed
First jump was a tandem that left me completely addicted to the rush. The moment of stepping out into nothing but air is unlike anything else.

Got my solo certification and started jumping regularly. Each jump teaches you something new about fear, trust, and letting go.

The freefall is just the beginning — under canopy is where you really learn to fly.

Motorcycling

15,000+ miles ridden
Started on back roads, learning to lean into turns and read the asphalt like a book. Every ride is a meditation in motion.

Took my first long-distance trip across state lines — just me, the bike, and hundreds of miles of open road ahead.

Motorcycling strips away everything unnecessary. It's pure connection between human, machine, and the road.

Scuba Diving

28 dives across 3 countries
First dive was in murky lake water, but even that glimpse of the underwater world was enough to hook me completely.

Got certified in tropical waters where the visibility stretched forever and the marine life was abundant.

Every dive is exploration of an alien world that exists right here on our planet.

Sailing

12 multi-day sailing trips
Learned to read the wind and work with forces much bigger than myself. Sailing teaches patience and respect for nature.

Nothing beats the silence of a boat cutting through water under full sail, powered only by the wind.

Each sailing trip is a lesson in self-reliance, weather reading, and finding your way by the stars.

Paramotoring

8 training flights completed
Discovered this sport as the closest thing to being a bird — just you, a wing, and a motor strapped to your back.

Training involved learning to control a parachute on the ground before ever adding the motor to the equation.

Flying low and slow gives you a completely different perspective than traditional aviation.

Skiing

25+ days on the mountain
First learned on the bunny slopes, falling more than skiing, but gradually found my rhythm on the mountain.

There's something magical about carving turns down a mountain with fresh powder flying behind you.

Skiing teaches you to read terrain, respect nature's power, and find flow in challenging conditions.

Kayaking

200+ miles paddled
First learned in calm lakes, but quickly moved to rivers where the water has personality and demands respect.

Each paddle stroke is a conversation with the current — sometimes you lead, sometimes you follow.

Kayaking gets you into places that boats can't reach and feet can't walk.
